🌧️ A Welcomed Relief: Breaking Down the Late March Rain Spell

A moderate to strong Western Disturbance is expected to affect Pakistan from around 27 March onward, moving eastward under the influence of the subtropical westerly jet and interacting with available moisture from the Arabian Sea. This system shows a well-organized vertical structure on ECMWF guidance, indicating a widespread precipitation event with a clear northwest to southeast gradient, where the highest accumulations are expected over northern and western parts of the country.

In Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and the northern regions (including Gilgit Baltistan and AJK), the system will remain dominant for a longer duration due to strong upper-level dynamics, positive vorticity advection, and orographic uplift. Expect widespread rain with embedded convection and snowfall over higher elevations. Accumulations should range around 80 to 150 mm, with some locations (like Dir, Swat, Chitral, Kohistan, and the GB ranges) potentially exceeding 150 to 200 mm. Moving towards upper and central Punjab (including Islamabad, Rawalpindi, and Lahore), frontal lifting will support moderate to locally heavy rainfall with totals generally between 40 to 80 mm. Central Punjab, including Faisalabad, may receive around 25 to 50 mm, with intensity gradually decreasing further south.

Over Balochistan, the system will initially impact the region through low-level convergence and terrain-induced uplift, bringing significant rainfall to northern and northeastern parts such as Quetta, Zhob, and Ziarat (50 to 100 mm expected, locally higher in pockets). Central areas may receive 30 to 60 mm, while southwestern parts including Turbat and Gwadar are likely to remain on the lower side (10 to 30 mm), though isolated thunderstorms remain possible. In Sindh, the impact will be comparatively weaker but still notable. Upper Sindh (Jacobabad, Sukkur, Larkana) is expected to see 30 to 60 mm, while central parts like Hyderabad and Nawabshah may receive 20 to 40 mm.

🌊
Karachi Outlook For Karachi, the situation remains more conditional as the city lies on the fringe of the system. However, current guidance indicates light to moderate rain or showers with chances of thunderstorms, especially during peak interaction phases (accumulations around 10 to 25 mm). Isolated higher pockets cannot be ruled out if convective activity manages to sustain. Overall, this Western Disturbance is likely to produce a countrywide precipitation event.

Pakistan Monsoon Outlook 2026

Analysis by Mudasir Jameel· Meteorological Research· 2026

Based on the latest models, the 2026 monsoon looks highly suppressed for the southern half of Pakistan. However, shifted monsoon trough dynamics will likely push excess moisture northward, favoring the upper regions.

Pacific Ocean Signal

Looking at the latest NINO3.4 SST anomaly plumes, temperatures are climbing steeply. Forecasts project anomalies pushing well past the +1.5°C threshold by July and August. This means a strong El Niño is actively forming. For Pakistan, a strong El Niño historically weakens the Walker Circulation, which severely reduces Arabian Sea moisture convergence and suppresses the cross-equatorial Somali Jet over Sindh and Balochistan.

Precipitation Outlook Breakdown

According to the latest probability models, the southern half of Pakistan (Sindh and Balochistan) is dominated by dry signals. However, during El Niño years, the monsoon trough frequently shifts northward toward the Himalayan foothills.

This shift, combined with mid-latitude westerly interactions, historically drives above-normal rainfall across Punjab, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Gilgit-Baltistan. These northern regions should remain on high alert for intense downpours and glacial melt flooding.

Regional Outlook

Sindh Below Normal

El Niño suppression will hit hardest here. A severe deficit is the leading scenario. Isolated intense events from Arabian Sea surges remain the only major rainfall hope.

S. Balochistan Below Normal

Monsoon penetration will be severely limited. The strong El Niño pattern sharply reduces both southwest monsoon incursions and late-summer systems.

Punjab Above Normal

Benefiting from shifted monsoon trough dynamics during El Niño, Punjab is expected to receive above-normal rainfall, particularly in the northern and central belts.

KPK Above Normal

Orographic lifting combined with increased westerly interactions will likely drive above-normal precipitation. High risk of flash flooding in mountainous terrains.
